Weak ankles? Do this quick routine three times a week.

First, calf raises. Rise onto your toes, then lower slowly. These build push-off strength for walking, running, and jumping.

Next, tibialis raises. Keep your heels down and lift your toes toward your shins. This strengthens the front of the lower leg and helps control your foot when you step.

Now use a resistance band in four directions: pull up, press down, turn inward, and turn outward. This trains the muscles that stabilize the ankle from every angle.

Next, single-leg balance. Keep your knee soft and your arch active. This builds control, coordination, and stability.

Then toe walks. Stay tall and walk on the balls of your feet to strengthen the calves, feet, and ankles together.

Now heel walks. Keep the toes lifted and walk on your heels to target the front of the lower leg.

Next, short-foot work. Pull the ball of the foot slightly toward the heel and raise the arch without curling your toes.

Finish with lateral step-downs. Lower slowly and keep the knee tracking over the toes. This teaches the ankle, knee, and hip to control movement together.

Move slow, stay controlled, and stop if pain gets sharp.
